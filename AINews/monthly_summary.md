# Monthly AI News Summary

# India's AI Boom

## Growth in India's App Market

* India's app market is experiencing significant growth, with in-app purchase revenue reaching over $300 million in Q1 2026, driven by non-gaming apps and generative AI.
* Global platforms are capturing most of the gains in India's app market, leaving Indian companies to compete with international players.
* The growth of India's app market is fueling an AI boom, with a focus on developing infrastructure to support the increasing demand for AI-driven applications.

## Indian IT Sector's Shift to AI

* At least 5 top Indian IT services firms, including Tata Consultancy Services, Infosys, and Wipro, have introduced or expanded AI-focused roles over the past year to adapt to the growing demand for automation.
* This shift in leadership and organizational structure suggests a structural change in the Indian IT sector in response to the increasing use of artificial intelligence (AI) in growth strategies.
* Other AI-related developments in the industry include the establishment of dedicated AI business units and AI-linked revenue disclosures among Indian IT services firms.

## TCS's AI-Driven Growth

* TCS, India's largest IT services company, reported a 12.22% rise in net profits for the March quarter, reaching Rs 13,718 crore.
* The profit surge is attributed to strategic investments in artificial intelligence (AI) and sustained customer confidence in technology investments.
* TCS is positioning itself strongly in the IT sector amidst changes in the technology landscape, driven by AI advances and workforce adjustments.

## AI Revolutionizing Indian Film Industry

* Indian film studios are using AI to revolutionize filmmaking, reducing production time and costs by up to 80%.
* AI-generated films and AI-assisted productions are becoming increasingly prevalent, with a Bollywood production house expecting AI-generated content to account for one-third of its revenue within three years.
* AI is being used for various applications, including dubbing movies into multiple languages and recutting endings of older titles to increase sales.

## Global AI Summit in India

* India is hosting the first-ever global AI summit in the Global South, focusing on sustainability and the environment, with themes like biodiversity conservation and natural resource management.
* AI is being used globally by governments and organizations to tackle climate change through mitigation, adaptation, and disaster risk resilience.
* AI is being used for various environmental tasks, including tracking deforestation, glacier retreat, and air quality.

## AI and India's Workforce

* India's tech workforce is facing significant challenges due to AI, with tens of thousands of job losses and a predicted displacement of up to 2 million jobs in the tech services sector by 2031.
* Despite the challenges, India is investing in AI skilling initiatives to prepare its workforce for emerging tech roles.
* An NITI Aayog report suggests that if India's workforce is strategically skilled, the number of jobs in the tech sector could swell by around 4 million in the next five years.

## Global AI Trends

* The US-China gap in AI computing power has narrowed to 2.7% according to Stanford's 423-page AI report.
* Global enterprise AI investment is expected to reach $581.7 billion in 2025, with the US accounting for nearly half of the investment.
* The number of AI researchers entering the US has seen a significant decline, dropping by 89% in 7 years and 80% in the past year.

## AI in Indian Agriculture

* India is leveraging AI to transform its agricultural ecosystem, with a focus on serving small and marginal farmers who have low digital literacy.
* Initiatives like AgriStack, Bharat-VISTAAR, and Agri Param are using AI to provide personalized, real-time advisory to farmers in their native languages, improving market engagement and access.
* The use of AI in agriculture is being driven by a combination of core companies, agrifintech startups, and large technology companies.

## AI and the Indian Workforce

* India Inc is facing a complex workforce landscape with rising employee expectations and cautious AI adoption, leading to a potential high churn rate.
* Despite concerns over AI surveillance, 79% of employees trust their organizations to equip them with AI skills, and there's a growing emphasis on collaboration and continuous upskilling.
* Organizations are being encouraged to redesign work, processes, and operating models to optimize human and machine capabilities.

## AI and Democracy

* Researchers warn that highly realistic AI-controlled personas could soon significantly influence democratic systems by subtly steering public opinion.
* Already, AI-generated deepfakes and fake news outlets have been detected in recent election conversations in countries such as the US, Taiwan, Indonesia, and India.
* The ability to detect fake AI images, particularly deepfakes of people, is becoming increasingly challenging, making it easier for AI swarms to potentially hijack democracy without being noticed.
